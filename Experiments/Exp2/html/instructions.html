Description

The experiment consists of three different phases, as illustrated in the figure below.
In the identical pre and post phases, you'll judge how similar different images of trees are from each other.
In the main task, you'll pretend to be a gardener who has to figure out which type of trees grow best in two different gardens.
There will be several breaks and detailed instructions will be provided once you go into full screen and click on the "start" button.





Full-screen requirement

We ask you to keep the window on fullscreen mode for the whole length of the experiment. This is because it is a psychological experiment and we need you to complete the experiment without distraction or doing other things at the same time. If you leave the fullscreen mode the experiment will be cancelled.

Duration and payment

During this experiment, you will need to give a fixed number of responses. You'll be doing an experiment with three different phases. The total duration of the experiment is estimated to be around 1 hour. The pre and post block of this experiment are identical and self-paced. The main experiment has a fixed duration but as we're allowing you to take breaks in between the different sub-blocks, you might need slightly more or less time in total.
Thus, the duration of the whole experiment depends on how quickly you respond, but we ask you to try your best, rather than just giving quick but careless responses!
Once you finish with the task, you will receive a code that you can paste in order for us to accept your HIT.
